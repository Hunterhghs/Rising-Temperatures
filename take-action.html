Take Action

The data is clear: urgent action is needed. But solutions exist — from policy levers to technological innovation to individual choices. Here's what's working and where progress is being made.

GLOBAL PROGRESS

Where We Stand

+1.2°C CURRENT WARMING

Tracking toward 1.5°C limit. At current rates, we reach it by ~2033.

37 Gt ANNUAL CO₂ EMISSIONS

Global emissions have plateaued but aren't yet declining. Peak expected by 2025–2027.

30% RENEWABLE ELECTRICITY

Share of global electricity from renewables. Solar and wind are the fastest-growing energy sources in history.

$1.8T CLEAN ENERGY INVESTMENT (2023)

Global clean energy investment surpassed fossil fuel investment for the first time in 2023.

WHAT WORKS

Proven Climate Solutions

solar_power

Renewable Energy Deployment

Solar PV costs have fallen 89% since 2010. Wind costs down 70%. Renewables are now the cheapest source of new electricity in most of the world. Tripling renewable capacity by 2030 is the single most impactful climate action.

electric_car

Transport Electrification

EV sales reached 18% of global car sales in 2023, up from 2% in 2018. Battery costs have fallen 97% since 1991. Norway leads at 93% EV market share. Full transition by 2035 in major markets.

forest

Natural Climate Solutions

Reforestation, peatland restoration, and improved agricultural practices can provide ~30% of needed emissions reductions by 2030 while delivering co-benefits for biodiversity, water, and food security.

engineering

Energy Efficiency

Buildings account for 40% of global emissions. Heat pumps, LED lighting, smart grids, and building retrofits offer some of the most cost-effective emissions reductions — often with net-negative costs over their lifetime.

POLICY LANDSCAPE

Key Climate Policies & Agreements

1

Paris Agreement (2015) — 195 Signatories

Legally binding international treaty to limit warming to well below 2°C, preferably 1.5°C. Countries submit Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) every 5 years. Current NDCs put the world on track for ~2.5°C — insufficient but improving.

2

EU Green Deal (2019) — Carbon Neutral by 2050

The European Union's comprehensive growth strategy targeting net-zero emissions by 2050. Includes Fit for 55 package, carbon border adjustment mechanism (CBAM), and the world's largest emissions trading system (EU ETS).

3

US Inflation Reduction Act (2022) — $369B Climate Investment

The largest climate investment in US history. Tax credits for renewable energy, EV manufacturing, hydrogen, carbon capture, and building efficiency. Projected to reduce US emissions 40% below 2005 levels by 2030.

4

Global Methane Pledge (2021) — 155 Countries

Commitment to reduce global methane emissions 30% below 2020 levels by 2030. Methane is 80× more potent than CO₂ over 20 years. Tackling methane is the fastest way to slow near-term warming.

PERSONAL IMPACT

What You Can Do

Individual actions matter — but systemic change is multiplicative. The most impactful actions combine personal choices with advocacy for policy change, corporate accountability, and community organizing.

campaign

Vote & Advocate

The single most impactful action: support candidates and policies that prioritize climate action. Write to representatives. Join climate organizations. Systemic change multiplies individual effort exponentially.

directions_bike

Transform Transport

Transport is the largest source of household emissions. Switch to an EV, use public transit, bike, walk, or reduce air travel. One fewer transatlantic flight saves ~1.6 tonnes CO₂.

energy_savings_leaf

Electrify Everything

Replace gas furnaces with heat pumps. Install solar panels. Switch to a renewable electricity provider. Induction cooktops instead of gas stoves. Electrification is the pathway to zero-emission homes.

restaurant

Shift Your Diet

Plant-based diets have 75% lower emissions than high-meat diets. Reducing food waste (which accounts for 8–10% of global emissions) is one of the most accessible climate actions anyone can take.

groups

Build Community

Join a local climate group. Start conversations. Share data and solutions. Community solar projects, local food cooperatives, and neighborhood resilience planning amplify impact beyond individual efforts.

paid

Align Your Investments

Move savings and pensions out of fossil fuels. ESG and climate-aware funds have grown to $30+ trillion. Divestment campaigns have shifted over $40 trillion in assets away from fossil fuels since 2012.
